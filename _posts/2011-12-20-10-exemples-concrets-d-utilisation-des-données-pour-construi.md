<p style="text-align: justify">Ces 10 exemples d'utilisation des données dans le domaine des transports de marchandises (déchets notamment) et des personnes, préfigurent les futurs outils d'aide qui seront orientés en fonction des utilisateurs : citoyen, ménage, collectivité, entreprise, état...</p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Traffic accidents in the U.S. between 2001 to 2009<br /> </strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong><a href="http://map.itoworld.com/road-casualties-usa" target="_blank">ITO-Road fatalities USA</a></strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong> <img alt="" height="298" src="http://international.ateneonaider.com/sites/default/files/17/1_0.jpg" width="449" /></strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify">An impressive work that collects all traffic accidents on different  roads of the United States by type of accident (pedestrian, driver,  year, etc..) And all in one map that has accumulated a huge range of  information for the period 2001 - 2009. The same team has prepared one  for the <a href="http://map.itoworld.com/road-casualties-uk" target="_blank">UK</a>. Guns of mass destruction? A silent tragedy? The map is shocking.</p> <p style="text-align: justify"> </p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The long journey of trash<br /> </strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><a href="http://senseable.mit.edu/trashtrack/" target="_blank">Trash track</a></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><img alt="" height="170" src="http://international.ateneonaider.com/sites/default/files/17/2.jpg" width="450" /> </p>  <!--more-->   <p style="text-align: justify">I wrote some lines about this project from MIT <a href="http://www.delicious.com/redirect?url=http%3A//www.ateneonaider.com/blog/manu-fernandez/trash-track-el-largo-viaje-de-la-basura" target="_blank">some time ago</a>.  What is worth watching in the video is how it explains the concept of  the project and the result of adding location aware tags to different  types of trash and see how each of them travel a huge amount of miles  until final disposal. Waste management and removal is an obscure and  secret system (throw away and forget about them) and the project helps  to visualize and understand there is a much more extensive life than we  imagine for the trash we throw away.</p> <p style="text-align: justify"> </p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong>A public hire bike system in real time<br /> </strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><a href="http://bikes.oobrien.com/?city=london" target="_blank">London Bike Share Map</a></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><img alt="" height="300" src="http://international.ateneonaider.com/sites/default/files/17/3.jpg" width="429" /></p> <p style="text-align: justify">This map visualizes all bikes of the public hire schemes in London.  From the same site, in fact, you can access and check other cities  (Zaragoza, Toronto, Lille, etc.). The project displays information on  the distribution of all the checkin points, the level of use at any  given time, temporal progression of use of each terminal and the  availability or not of bicycles at each point.</p> <p style="text-align: justify"> </p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The intense activity of a subway network</strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><a href="http://graphicsweb.wsj.com/documents/MTAFARES1108/#v=showCommuters" target="_blank">Examining MetroCard usage</a></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><img alt="" height="243" src="http://international.ateneonaider.com/sites/default/files/17/4.jpg" width="440" /></p> <p style="text-align: justify">What to do with the data from every user entries in the extensive  network of subway in New York? This phenomenal work published by Wall  Street Journal is a good example of how to use information from  seemingly irrelevant individual data: types of tickets, stations,  schedules, fares, etc. Put this in a map and add logic to the data to  understand, among other things, the variation in use according to the  tariff changes introduced in the price system.</p> <p style="text-align: justify"> </p> <p style="text-align: justify">Real-time use of bicycles</p> <p style="text-align: justify"><a href="http://oliverobrien.co.uk/2011/02/flow-animation-of-barclays-cycle-hire-bikes/" target="_blank">London Hire Bikes animation</a></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><img alt="" height="282" src="http://international.ateneonaider.com/sites/default/files/17/5.jpg" width="440" /></p> <p style="text-align: justify">Another one about bikes. The <a href="http://vimeo.com/19486470" target="_blank">video </a>shows  the flow dynamically of the bicycles used moving through the 18 hours  of the day. I also mentioned this and other projects about London <a href="http://www.ateneonaider.com/blog/manu-fernandez/visualizaci%C3%B3n-de-datos-urbanos-londres-en-taxi-bici-y-bus" target="_blank">some weeks ago</a>.</p> <p style="text-align: justify"> </p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong>A U.S. map block by block</strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><a href="http://projects.nytimes.com/census/2010/explorer" target="_blank">Mapping America: Every City, Every Block</a></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><img alt="" height="268" src="http://international.ateneonaider.com/sites/default/files/17/6_0.jpg" width="440" /></p> <p style="text-align: justify">What can you do with the census data? With this map you can reach the  level of detail of every building anywhere in the country and see the  distribution of population by race, by income, by type of household,  type of housing or education, and understand the dynamics of spatial  distribution at national, regional, urban or neighborhood level.</p> <p style="text-align: justify"> </p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Time distance to get around the city<br /> </strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><a href="http://shop.mapumental.com/samples/" target="_blank">Mapumental</a></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><img alt="" height="353" src="http://international.ateneonaider.com/sites/default/files/17/7.jpg" width="353" /></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><a href="http://www.mysociety.org/projects/mapumental/" target="_blank">MySociety </a>developed  years ago this project that perfectly illustrates the utility of  georeferenced data. Mapumental tool displays the travel time to reach a  certain point from anywhere in the city, thereby helping to understand  the temporal distance mobility, a much more useful and practical  information than just physical distance.</p> <p style="text-align: justify"> </p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The changing city. Day and night<br /> </strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><a href="http://www.joelertola.com/grfx/population/d_n.html" target="_blank">Day vs. Night population maps</a></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><img alt="" height="214" src="http://international.ateneonaider.com/sites/default/files/17/8.jpg" width="448" /></p> <p style="text-align: justify">A simple but powerful idea. The population of New York during the day and at night, reflecting the density of different areas.</p> <p style="text-align: justify"> </p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Singapore real time<br /> </strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><a href="http://senseable.mit.edu/livesingapore/index.html" target="_blank">LIVE Singapore!</a></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><img alt="" height="335" src="http://international.ateneonaider.com/sites/default/files/17/9.jpg" width="445" /></p> <p style="text-align: justify">Another well-known MIT project from Seansable City Lab. Using  different data sets and maps designed to explain the impact of rain on  the level of use of the taxi in the city, predicted travel time based on  changing traffic conditions , the heat island effect or continuous flow  of arrivals and departures of peopl

e and goods in a city that serves as  a hub of the global economy. The <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2aEPkyOBtRo&feature=player_embedded" target="_blank">video </a>explains it all.</p> <p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Understanding air pollution<br /> </strong></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><a href="http://www.intheair.es/" target="_blank">In the air</a></p> <p style="text-align: justify"><img alt="" height="212" src="http://international.ateneonaider.com/sites/default/files/17/10.jpg" width="450" /></p> <p style="text-align: justify">Make visible the invisible dirty air we breathe, nothing less. That's what Nerea Calvillo proposed in a <a href="http://intheair.es/app/index.html" target="_blank">dynamic </a>model to visualize and map the footprint of air pollution in Madrid.</p>
